🌐 SOFTWARE SPECIFICATION BRIEF: "SAVE THE WATERS" PLATFORM

1. Project Overview & Tech Stack

"Save the Waters" is an enterprise-grade IoT environmental monitoring and
advocacy platform designed to track water levels and quality across Lagos
State's waterfronts, using Makoko as the primary active case study.

Recommended Tech Stack (For the Developer)

  - Frontend Framework: Next.js (React) — chosen for seamless Vercel hosting,
    SEO optimization, and fast routing.
  - Styling Engine: Tailwind CSS — for clean, responsive, and modern layout
    structures.
  - GIS Engine: Leaflet.js (via react-leaflet) — open-source, lightweight
    mapping engine with no usage fees or credit card requirements.
  - Animation Library: Framer Motion — for smooth, hardware-accelerated water
    level animations.
  - Data Visualization: Recharts or Chart.js — for displaying ThingSpeak
    historical trend graphs.

2. Page-by-Page Specifications

Page 1: The Landing Page (Advocacy & Awareness)

UI Style Guidelines: Clean, high-impact, modern, using a color palette and
layout hierarchy inspired by the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
website. Wide containers, bold typography, and cards with large, clear icons.

Key Sections:

1.  The Hero Section:
      - A high-definition background image of Lagos waters (contrast between raw
        nature and urban waterfronts).
      - Main Copy: "Protecting Nigeria's Waterfronts: Real-time IoT surveillance
        for climate resilience and ecological safety."
      - A prominent, custom-styled CTA button: "Explore Live Map" (links
        directly to Page 2).
2.  Advocacy Campaign — "Bring Back Lagos Marine Life":
      - A highly engaging, story-driven layout focusing on the ecological impact
        of pollution.
      - Educational Copy: Explaining how native marine life (like dolphins and
        whales, once common in Lagos waters) left due to uncontrolled chemical
        dumping, dredging noise, and sewage.
      - The Solution: Explaining how our IoT sensor network collects hard,
        un-fakeable data to help local environmental agencies (like LASEPA)
        enforce ISO corporate compliance and stop toxic industrial waste at the
        source, paving the way for marine ecosystems to heal and aquatic life to
        return.
      - Interactive Call-to-Action: A "Join the Initiative" email signup or
        petition link.

Page 2: The Interactive GIS Map (Lagos Monitoring Grid)

A full-screen Map Page that visualizes the distribution of monitoring nodes
across Lagos Lagoon.

Map Features:

1.  Default Viewport: Center coordinates [6.5050, 3.4350] (covering the Lagos
    Lagoon, Third Mainland Bridge, Lekki, and Ikorodu) with a zoom level of 12.
2.  Multi-Device Pins: Place pins at:
      - Makoko (Case Study): Coordinates [6.4965, 3.3963] (Labeled: LIVE GPRS
        FEED).
      - Lekki Phase 1: Coordinates [6.4468, 3.4619] (Labeled: Simulated Demo
        Station).
      - Ikorodu Terminal: Coordinates [6.5910, 3.5042] (Labeled: Simulated Demo
        Station).
3.  Interaction:
      - Hovering over a pin shows a custom Leaflet tooltip with the Station Name
        and Online/Offline status.
      - Clicking a pin slides open the Detailed Station Dashboard (Page 3) for
        that specific node.

Section A: Water Level Animation (Framer Motion)

  - The Visualization: A highly clean, minimal vector graphic of a standard
    mid-size sedan car (simulating a real-world vehicle parked on a Lagos
    street).
  - The Animation: A blue-tinted overlay representing the water level. This
    overlay rises and falls dynamically based on the current sensor
    distanceValue reading (0 to 100 cm).
  - The Dynamic Color Coding (As specified by the user):
      - Clear Blue Water: Normal/Safe condition (water is far from the sensor,
        distanceValue > 30cm). Text overlay reads: "Status: Safe (Ecosystem
        Normal)".
      - Yellow Water: Warning/Too Low condition (water is drying out or sensor
        is misreading, distanceValue > 150cm). Text overlay reads: "Warning:
        Level Depleted / Out of Range".
      - Green Water: Danger/Too High condition (water has breached the safety
        threshold and is flooding, distanceValue < 30cm). The water overlay
        rises up the car wheels/doors. Text overlay flashes: "DANGER: FLOODING
        DETECTED".

Section B: The pH Level Gauge

  - The Visualization: A radial gauge or vertical meter showing the current pH
    scale from 0 to 14 with a floating indicator pointing to the exact decimal
    value.
  - Dynamic Translation Box (What it means): Next to the gauge, a text block
    automatically updates to translate the raw pH number into a real-world
    warning:
      - pH < 6.5 (Red Text): "Unsafe Acidic Level (Suspected industrial chemical
        runoff or organic decay)."
      - pH 6.5 – 8.5 (Green Text): "Ecosystem Optimal (Safe for marine life,
        human contact, and local aquaculture)."
      - pH > 8.5 (Red Text): "Unsafe Alkaline Level (Suspected raw domestic
        sewage or heavy soap/detergent contamination)."

Section C: Historical Data Charts

  - Embedding of the ThingSpeak iframe charts (Field 1 and Field 2) showing the
    fluctuating trends over the last 24 hours/week to allow researchers to
    download the data as a CSV sheet for environmental reports.

3. Recommended Extra Features (Value Additions)

To elevate this project even further for the final defense, have your developer
add these sections:

1.  "Report a Violation" (ISO Portal): A simple form where local residents or
    fishermen can upload pictures of illegal industrial dumping in their area.
    This data is mapped directly on the GIS grid to identify illegal corporate
    polluters.
2.  Public Health Warnings: A dynamic block that uses the current pH and water
    level to display active safety recommendations (e.g., "Current water
    conditions are unsafe. High risk of waterborne pathogens (Cholera). Avoid
    contact with skin.").

4. API Integration Details for the Developer

  - ThingSpeak API Endpoint:
    https://api.thingspeak.com/channels/${channelId}/feeds/last.json?api_key=${readApiKey}
  - Data Parsing:
      - field1 represents the raw distanceValue in centimeters (used to drive
        the car water level animation height).
      - field2 represents the raw pHValue (used to drive the pH gauge indicator
        and the dynamic warning text).
